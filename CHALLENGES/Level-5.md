## 🎯 Objective

Become a pillar of the DevQuest community. Help others, lead by example, and make lasting contributions that empower future questers.

**Prerequisite**: Complete [Level 4 — Open Source Explorer](Level-4.md) and reach 🥈 **Collaborator** rank (600 XP).

---

## 📋 Requirements

To complete Level 5, you must accomplish **ALL THREE** of the following over a rolling 60-day period:

### 🅐 Mentor a Beginner

Help at least **3 different newcomers** complete their Level 1 or Level 2 challenges by:
- Leaving detailed, helpful review comments on their PRs
- Answering their questions in GitHub Discussions or Issues
- Guiding them through fixes without doing it for them

### 🅑 Make a Significant Contribution

Make at least **one substantial contribution** to DevQuest or another major open source project:
- Add a new learning resource or challenge
- Implement a GitHub Action workflow
- Significantly improve existing documentation
- Create a tool or script that helps contributors

### 🅒 Review at Least 5 Pull Requests

Review PRs in DevQuest with quality feedback:
- Explain *why* changes are needed, not just *what*
- Be encouraging and respectful
- Approve PRs that meet standards
- Your reviews should help the author learn

## 📖 How to Be a Great Mentor

### The Golden Rule of Mentoring

> *Don't give fish — teach to fish.*

Instead of: *"Change line 3 to X"*

Say: *"This line has a formatting issue. Check our style guide at [link] — can you see what needs to change? Happy to clarify!"*

### Effective Review Comments

```markdown
# ❌ Unhelpful
"This is wrong."

# ✅ Helpful
"The username entry on line 47 looks like it might be out of alphabetical order —
the contributor above you starts with 'R' and you start with 'S', so your entry
should come after theirs. Take a look at the table and let me know if you have
questions!"
```

### Encouraging New Contributors

- Acknowledge the effort: *"Thanks for opening this PR!"*
- Be specific with feedback
- Offer to help: *"Feel free to ask if you're stuck"*
- Celebrate milestones: *"You've got it — nice work!"*

## 📖 Reviewing Pull Requests

### The Review Checklist

When reviewing a PR, check:

```
Content Quality
 □ Is the addition accurate and helpful?
 □ Does it follow the existing style?
 □ Are there typos or grammatical errors?

Format & Structure
 □ Correct markdown formatting?
 □ Proper emoji usage?
 □ Links working?
 □ Alphabetical order (for contributor lists)?

Process
 □ Correct branch name?
 □ Proper commit message format?
 □ PR title follows conventions?
 □ PR description filled in?
```

### How to Submit a Review

1. Open a PR in DevQuest
2. Click **"Files changed"** tab
3. Hover over lines to add line comments
4. Click **"Review changes"** to submit
5. Choose **Comment**, **Approve**, or **Request changes**

## 💡 Tips

> **Tip 1**: Consistency beats intensity. Spending 30 minutes mentoring each day for a month is more valuable than a single 8-hour session.

> **Tip 2**: Keep track of all your mentoring activities from day 1. It's easy to forget what you did three weeks ago.

> **Tip 3**: The best time to review a PR is within 24 hours of it being opened. Fresh PRs get momentum faster.

> **Tip 4**: Don't hesitate to tag maintainers if you're unsure whether to approve or request changes. Asking is always better than guessing.

> **Tip 5**: Your significant contribution doesn't need to be code. A thorough, well-written guide has helped thousands of developers.
